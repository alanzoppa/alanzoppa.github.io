🤖 AI Coding Benchmarks — April 2026

Researched by ollama/kimi-k2.6:cloud · April 27, 2026

Key Takeaways

  • GPT-5.5 takes the SWE-bench Pro crown: Achieves 58.6% on SWE-bench Pro and a state-of-the-art 82.7% on Terminal-Bench 2.0, establishing itself as the leader for agentic coding and DevOps workflows.
  • Claude Opus 4.7 emerges as the reliability champion: Anthropic's "xhigh" effort level delivers 64.3% on SWE-bench Pro with dramatically improved instruction following and tool-use consistency, making it the top choice for complex, long-running tasks.
  • Kimi K2.6 becomes the new open-weight king: With 80.2% SWE-bench Verified (self-reported), 1T total/32B active parameters, and a 256K context window, it replaces K2.5 as the top open-source coding model at just $0.38/$1.72 per 1M tokens.
  • Gemini 3.1 Pro still leads on verified SWE-bench: Maintains the top third-party verified score of 78.8% on SWE-bench Verified and 2887 Elo on LiveCodeBench, proving Google's continued dominance in coding benchmarks.
  • Qwen 3.6 Plus jumps to 1M context with enterprise focus: Alibaba's latest adds repository-level engineering with a 1M-token context window and built-in tool integration, targeting enterprise agentic workflows.
  • The price-performance gap keeps widening: Gemma 4 31B at $0.13/$0.38 is now 192x cheaper per token than GPT-5.5 Pro ($30/$180), while MiniMax M2.7 delivers 73.8% SWE-bench Verified at just $0.30/$1.20.
  • Terminal-Bench 2.0 is the new DevOps standard: GPT-5.5's 82.7% and Opus 4.7's 69.4% establish a clear hierarchy for CLI and infrastructure tasks that didn't exist a month ago.
